Camera scanning tips
  • Hold phone 6–12 inches away with the barcode filling most of the frame.
  • Keep the barcode flat, clean, and well lit — no shadows.
  • Avoid glare — rotate slightly to eliminate reflections.
  • Try rotating the phone sideways for long 1D barcodes.
  • On iPhone: switch to 1D Barcode mode for product/material labels.
  • Thermal receipt barcodes (low contrast or wrinkled) — use manual entry instead.

Manual / Handheld Scanner Input
Type a code or connect a handheld scanner. Most handheld scanners type the code and press Enter automatically — no extra steps needed.
Best field workflow
  1. Use WSF QR labels when available — fastest and most reliable.
  2. Use a handheld scanner or manual input for stubborn 1D barcodes.
  3. Use camera scan for QR codes and clean product barcodes.
For iPhone 1D barcodes, choose 1D Barcode mode above. Receipts may still be unreliable — thermal receipt barcodes are often low-contrast or distorted. In those cases, manual entry is the right call.
